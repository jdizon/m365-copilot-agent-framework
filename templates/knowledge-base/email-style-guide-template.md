## Email length guidelines

| Context | Target length | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Quick acknowledgement | 2-4 sentences | No need for full context recap |
| Status update | 1-3 short paragraphs | Bullet points for action items |
| Formal response | 3-5 paragraphs | Include context, response, next steps |
| Escalation | Short and direct | State the issue, what is needed, by when |
| Meeting follow-up | 1-2 paragraphs | Summary + action items with owners |

---

## Tone guidelines

| Situation | Tone | Avoid |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Routine update | Friendly, direct | Overly formal language |
| Client request | Professional, responsive | Vague commitments |
| Blocker / escalation | Assertive, factual | Apologetic language unless warranted |
| Decision confirmation | Clear, authoritative | Ambiguous phrasing |
| FYI / informational | Brief, matter-of-fact | Unnecessary padding |

## Common phrases (approved)

**Acknowledging receipt:**
- "Thank you for your email."
- "Thanks for flagging this."
- "Received — we'll look into this."

**Committing to action:**
- "I'll follow up by [date]."
- "We'll have this resolved by [date]."
- "I'll loop in [Name] to assist with this."

**Requesting information:**
- "Could you please provide [specific detail]?"
- "To progress this, we'll need [X] from you."

**Closing an item:**
- "This has been resolved — please let us know if you need anything further."
- "Happy to assist if you have any other questions."

## What the agent should NOT do when drafting

- Do not make commitments to specific dates without checking availability
- Do not disclose internal project codes or system names to external recipients
- Do not use overly casual language (slang, emoji) in client-facing emails
- Do not apologise excessively — one acknowledgement is enough
- Do not copy people not already on the thread without instruction
